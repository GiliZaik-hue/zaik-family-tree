The great migration: from the Pale of Settlement to Eretz Israel

The Zaik family's story is woven from the broader tapestry of Jewish emigration from the Russian Empire. Aryeh Zaik, born in Odessa, Ukraine, and his wife Lea Leika, born in Tiraspol, Moldova, were among the families who made the journey to Rishon LeTsiyon — one of the first modern Jewish settlements in Israel.

The connected Goldin family arrived from the Kovno Province of Lithuania, with branches in Telsai, Keidan, and Vilna. The Gurevitch, Himelfarb, Volchinsky, Averbuch, and Sinyaver families came from the Ukrainian cities of Odessa, Berdychiv, Kherson, Mykolaiv, and Smila — part of the vast wave of Jewish aliyah that shaped modern Israel. Some branches, like the Goldins, also sent family members westward to Philadelphia, Baltimore, and Newport, tracing the twin paths of Jewish emigration in that era.

The family put down roots in Israel — in Rishon LeTsiyon, Tel Aviv, Ramat Gan, Rehovot, and Petah Tiqwa — with the youngest generation growing up as native Israelis.

Family branches

Zaik branch

From Odessa to Rishon LeTsiyon

The Zaik family, rooted in Odessa and Tiraspol, were among the early settlers of Rishon LeTsiyon — one of the first Zionist agricultural settlements, founded in 1882. Aryeh and Lea Zaik's descendants have lived there across five generations.

Goldin branch

Lithuania to Israel, America & England

The Goldin family from Vilna, Telsai, and Keidan (Kėdainiai), Lithuania dispersed in three directions: Nachum Goldin settled in Rishon LeTsiyon alongside the Zaiks; Chaya Sarah and Rivka Beckie emigrated to America and England; while others remained connected to the Lithuanian Jewish world.

Averbuch–Berniskai branch

Tiraspol & Smila to Tel Aviv

Zeev Averbuch (from Tiraspol) and Genia née Berniskai (from Smila, Ukraine) met and built their family in Tel Aviv. Their daughter Naomi lived in Rishon LeTsiyon, bridging the Averbuch and Zaik circles.

Volchinsky–Himelfarb branch

Kherson & Berdychiv to Petah Tiqwa

The Volchinsky family from Nikolaev (Mykolaiv) and the Himelfarb family from Berdychiv and Odessa converged in Petah Tiqwa, Israel. Aryeh Volchinsky married Rachel Himelfarb, uniting two Ukrainian-origin families in the heart of Israel.

Sinyaver–Reifman branch

Odessa to Tel Aviv & Ramat Gan

Avraham Sinyaver emigrated from Odessa to Tel Aviv. His daughter Ora Sinyaver and her husband Uri Reifman built their family in Ramat Gan.

Flynn branch

England to Ramat Gan & Rishon LeTsiyon

Frank Leo Flynn, born in England, is the patriarch of the Israeli Flynn family. His son Gabi, born in Ramat Gan, married Adina Zaik — joining the English and Zionist pioneer heritages into a single Israeli family.
